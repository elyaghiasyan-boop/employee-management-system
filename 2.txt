2. Global Labour Force Metrics (ILOSTAT)
The ILOSTAT database (ILO’s comprehensive labour statistics platform) provides harmonized data for ~189 countries, including: ILOSTAT
Key Global Indicators (2024–2025)
Metric	World Estimate
Labour force participation rate	~61.0 % International Labour Organization

Unemployment rate	~5.0 % International Labour Organization

Working poverty rate	~6.9 % International Labour Organization

Informal employment share	~57.8 % International Labour Organization

These figures are global estimates that include developed and developing economies, and can be disaggregated by region, sex, age group, and income group using the ILOSTAT data tools. ILOSTAT

4. Wages, Earnings & Wage Growth
OECD Wage Trends (2025 Outlook)
•	Real wage growth (average) across OECD: ~2.5 % in early 2025. OECD
•	Minimum wage increases: real minimum wages rose ~7.9 % on average across OECD countries with statutory minimum wages. OECD
•	Wages for lowest paid workers showed resilience compared with median wages post pandemic. OECD

5. Additional High Resolution Data Sources
For deeper data that includes country by country statistics across many indicators:
ILOSTAT Detailed Datasets (Free)
ILOSTAT allows download of >250M data points for indicators such as: ILOSTAT
•	Labour force participation rate by age/sex per country
•	Employment to population ratio
•	Unemployment rate by country (and youth vs adult)
•	Informal employment rates
•	Earnings and monthly employee earnings
•	Detailed datasets in CSV via bulk download
This is ideal for full global tables and time series. ILOSTAT
World Bank Gender Disaggregated Labour Database (GDLD)
The GDLD dataset includes:
•	Detailed employment by occupation and sector
•	Wage and income data by sex
•	Micro level harmonized labour force stats from >100 countries Data Topics

ILOSTAT indicators (annual values per country) such as:
•	Labour force participation
•	Employment rates
•	Unemployment
•	Working poverty
•	Earnings indicators
OECD Employment Data (for high income detailed labour market structure):
•	Employment by status (full/part–time)
•	Job tenure, job status
•	Hours worked distribution & earnings deciles
World Bank GDLD:
•	Wage and income distributions
•	Gender wage gaps
•	Sectoral employment breakdowns
